    • ABOUT ME

    I started my yoga journey on a whim about five years ago. At the time I was struggling with mental health and yoga was suggested to me as a way to find mindful calm and ease. I had always heard of these rumored benefits but never quite understood them; I equivocated yoga with stretching which was not physical enough to really tout any significant advantages. However, after my first class I soon realized that not only is yoga physically challenging, it's extremely intimidating! There were pose names that I didn't know and people balancing on their arms when I could barely hold a plank. I felt silly and uncomfortable during meditation and I made so many countless mistakes during the pose sequence that I was certain my amateur abilities were on full display for everyone to mock. Regardless of my incessant overthinking during that class, I absolutely loved the mental challenge of listening to my body.

    From that moment forward I was inspired to start a consistent yoga practice. My passion for yoga drove me to get certified in June 2019 so that I could continually share this practice with others and instruct with anatomical safety first and foremost. I truly believe that yoga should be inclusive of everyone and I work t o cultivate a welcoming atmosphere to minimize any intimidation. Over time I've learned that there's no such thing as being 'good' or 'bad' at yoga ‐ varying levels simply exist through development on a micro level each day.

    While I won't promise that you will experience the same benefits of yoga or discover the same meaning behind it like I did, I'd love to be a part of your personal journey as one of the countless guides available to you.
